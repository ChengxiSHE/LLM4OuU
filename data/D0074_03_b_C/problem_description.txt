A logistics company needs to determine daily freight capacity allocation based on fluctuating market rates. The decision variable is the daily freight capacity quantity allocated for transportation operations. The objective is to maximize total expected profit by balancing revenue and operating costs, where revenue equals allocated capacity multiplied by the uncertain daily opening freight rate, and cost equals allocated capacity multiplied by a fixed operating cost of $0.05 per unit. The daily allocated capacity must not exceed the company's maximum transportation capability of 100 units. The daily opening freight rate is uncertain and directly affects revenue calculation in the objective function.
