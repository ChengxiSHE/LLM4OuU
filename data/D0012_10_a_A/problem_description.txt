Batch ordering of crops is an important agricultural issue that requires companies to purchase agricultural products in one go before the crops mature. The goal of a company is to plan the pre purchase quantity as accurately as possible in the following period of time T. When the planned quantity is too large, it can lead to stockout losses, and when the planned quantity is too small, it can lead to overflow losses, and both have the same proportion in the objective function, with a weight coefficient of 1. The decision of a company is to plan the quantity of crops to be purchased each period of time T. Meanwhile, the quantity of crop production capacity is unknown.
