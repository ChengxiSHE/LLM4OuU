The company needs to determine weekly procurement quantities for a product based on historical price patterns in the logistics market. The decision variable is the weekly procurement quantity of the product, planned for the upcoming 4 weeks. The objective is to maximize total expected profit, where profit equals total revenue minus total procurement cost and inventory holding cost. Revenue is calculated as sales quantity multiplied by a constant selling price of $450 per unit, with sales quantity limited by market absorption capacity. Procurement cost equals procurement quantity multiplied by the uncertain weekly highest price. The weekly procurement quantity must be non-negative and cannot exceed a weekly capacity of 100 units. Additionally, inventory holding cost is incurred for carried-over stock at a rate of $20 per unit per week, and total inventory cannot exceed 200 units due to storage constraints. The initial inventory is 50 units. The weekly highest price of the product is uncertain, derived from historical data. The goal is to determine an optimal procurement strategy that balances revenue opportunities against procurement costs and inventory expenses under price uncertainty.
