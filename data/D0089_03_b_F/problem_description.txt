A bakery must determine weekly hamburger bun production quantities to maximize profitability. The decision variable is the weekly production quantity of hamburger buns planned for the upcoming month. The objective is to maximize total expected profit, where profit equals total revenue minus total cost. Revenue is calculated as sales quantity multiplied by the uncertain weekly average market price of hamburger buns, where sales quantity is the minimum of production quantity plus beginning inventory and the uncertain weekly demand. Total cost comprises production cost (production quantity multiplied by a fixed production cost of $100 per bun) and holding cost (ending inventory multiplied by a holding cost coefficient of $20 per bun). The weekly production quantity must be non-negative, with a minimum production requirement of 500 buns per week to maintain operational efficiency, and cannot exceed the bakery's maximum weekly capacity of 10,000 buns. Additionally, the inventory capacity is limited to 2,000 buns. The uncertain parameters are the weekly average market price of hamburger buns and the weekly demand, both following distributions estimated from historical data. The goal is to find an optimal production strategy that balances revenue opportunities against production and inventory costs under market uncertainty.
