A vegetable wholesaler must determine weekly leek procurement quantities to maximize profitability. The decision variable is the weekly procurement quantity of leeks, planned for the upcoming 4 weeks. The objective is to maximize total expected profit, where profit equals total revenue minus total cost, with revenue calculated as procurement quantity multiplied by a constant selling price of $750 per unit, and total cost comprising procurement cost (procurement quantity multiplied by the uncertain weekly average leek price with a weight of 1) and holding cost (procurement quantity multiplied by a holding cost coefficient of 0.5). The weekly procurement quantity must be non-negative and must not exceed 1000 units due to storage limitations. The weekly average leek price is uncertain and directly influences the procurement cost in the objective function.
