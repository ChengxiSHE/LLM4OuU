A financial institution needs to acquire a total of 100,000 shares of MasterCard stock over the next 30 trading days to build a strategic equity position. The institution must determine the daily purchase quantities to complete this acquisition while managing price volatility risks.
The decision variable is the daily quantity of MasterCard shares to purchase.
The objective is to minimize the risk-adjusted total purchase cost, measured by minimizing the Conditional Value-at-Risk (CVaR) at the 95% confidence level. This goal balances the expected total expenditure against the extreme cost volatility risk derived from historical MasterCard stock price movements.
The daily purchase quantity must be non-negative and cannot exceed 5,000 shares per day due to market impact considerations. Additionally, the purchasing strategy must maintain total expenditure below $40 million and ensure the 95% Budget-at-Risk remains under $2.5 million throughout the acquisition period.
The uncertain parameter is the daily closing price of MasterCard stock. The historical opening and closing price data are used to model the uncertainty in future price movements and to estimate the volatility for risk assessment in the optimization.
