Farmers must purchase seeds weekly to maximize profitability, but the weekly seed price is uncertain. The decision variable is the weekly seed purchase quantity, planned for each week of the upcoming month. The objective is to maximize the expected profit minus a risk-adjusted term that accounts for the variance of profit, where profit equals total revenue minus total purchasing cost. Revenue is calculated as purchase quantity multiplied by a constant selling price of $3500 per unit, and cost equals purchase quantity multiplied by the uncertain weekly seed price. The weekly seed purchase quantity must be non-negative, with a minimum purchase requirement of 50 units per week to ensure sufficient supply for planting. Additionally, a limited total budget of $800,000 is available for seed purchases across the month, which must be adhered to in expectation. The risk aversion parameter λ is set to 0.1. The seed price for each week is represented by historical data, with the price distribution assumed to be stochastic based on past trends.
