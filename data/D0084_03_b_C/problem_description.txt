The company needs to determine daily procurement quantities for a supply chain product to manage price volatility. The decision variable is the daily procurement quantity, planned for the next 30 days. The objective is to maximize the total expected profit minus a risk-adjusted term that accounts for the variance of profit, where profit equals total revenue minus total procurement cost and minus total inventory holding cost. Revenue is calculated as procurement quantity multiplied by a constant selling price of $0.15 per unit. Procurement cost is calculated as the sum of procurement quantities multiplied by the uncertain daily highest prices. The daily procurement quantity must be non-negative and cannot exceed a daily capacity of 100 units. Additionally, the inventory capacity is limited to 500 units, and a holding cost of $0.005 per unit per day is applied to the ending inventory. The initial inventory is 100 units. The daily highest price is uncertain and based on historical data. The risk aversion parameter λ is set to 0.001. The goal is to find an optimal procurement strategy that balances expected profit against the risks of price volatility while respecting capacity and inventory constraints.
