A logistics manager must determine optimal safety stock levels to mitigate replenishment delays for a fast-moving consumer good. The decision variable is the daily safety stock quantity maintained to buffer against lead time variability. The objective is to minimize total cost, expressed as a linear function with a holding cost coefficient of 5 per unit and a stockout cost coefficient of 10 per unit. The daily safety stock quantity must be non-negative. The replenishment lead time is uncertain.
