An investor needs to fully allocate a total investment capital of $150,000 to AAPL stock over the next 30 trading days, considering uncertain price movements. The investor must determine the daily investment amounts to deploy the entire capital optimally while balancing potential returns against investment risks.
The decision variable is the daily investment amount in AAPL stock.
The objective is to maximize the risk-adjusted total return, measured by maximizing the Sharpe ratio using a 2% annualized risk-free rate. This goal balances the expected total return against the return volatility risk derived from historical AAPL daily price movements.
The daily investment amount must be non-negative and cannot exceed $10,000 due to trading limitations. Additionally, the investment strategy must maintain maximum drawdown below 12% and ensure the 95% Value-at-Risk remains under $12,000 throughout the investment period.
The uncertain parameter is the daily return of AAPL stock, derived from opening and closing prices. The historical price data is used to model the uncertainty in future price movements and to estimate the volatility for risk-adjusted return optimization.
