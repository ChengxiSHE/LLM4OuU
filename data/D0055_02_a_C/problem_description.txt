The power station must plan daily energy generation to meet uncertain demand while minimizing operational costs over the next 30 days.  
The decision variable is the daily energy production level, which must be scheduled for each of the 30 days.
The objective is to minimize total generation cost, comprising a generation cost coefficient of $50 per MWh and a penalty coefficient of $100 per MWh for any shortage.
The daily energy production must be non-negative and cannot exceed the station's maximum capacity of 20 MWh per day.
The actual daily power consumption is uncertain and affects the cost calculation.
