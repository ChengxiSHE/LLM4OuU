A flower market aims to optimize weekly Gotukola procurement to maximize profitability. The decision variable is the weekly procurement quantity of Gotukola, planned for each week over the next month. The objective is to maximize total expected profit, where profit equals revenue minus total cost. Revenue is calculated as sales quantity multiplied by the uncertain weekly average selling price of Gotukola, where sales quantity is the minimum of procurement quantity and the uncertain weekly demand. Total cost includes purchase cost (procurement quantity multiplied by a fixed procurement price of $40 per unit) and holding cost (ending inventory multiplied by a holding cost coefficient of $5 per unit). The weekly procurement quantity must be non-negative, with a minimum procurement requirement of 50 units per week to ensure supply continuity, and cannot exceed the storage capacity of 1000 units. The weekly average selling price and weekly demand are uncertain, with historical data provided for price. The goal is to determine an optimal procurement strategy that balances revenue opportunities against purchase and holding costs under demand and price uncertainty.
