The company needs to set weekly selling prices for a product in a specific market to maximize profit. The decision variable is the weekly price set by the company for the product over a planning horizon of 4 weeks. The objective is to maximize the total expected profit minus a risk-adjusted term that accounts for profit variability, where profit equals total revenue minus total procurement cost. Revenue is calculated as sales quantity multiplied by the set price, and cost is calculated as sales quantity multiplied by a fixed procurement cost of $100 per unit. The sales quantity each week is influenced by the company's price relative to the market's lowest price: setting a higher price typically reduces sales, while the market's lowest price establishes the competitive benchmark. The weekly price must be non-negative and cannot exceed a maximum price of $300 to maintain market viability. Additionally, the price change between consecutive weeks is limited to ensure pricing stability. The company must also maintain a minimum sales volume of 50 units per week to sustain market presence. The market's lowest selling price each week is uncertain and based on historical data. The goal is to determine an optimal pricing strategy that balances revenue generation against procurement costs and market competition, while managing the risks associated with price volatility.
