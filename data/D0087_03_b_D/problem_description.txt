A logistics company must determine weekly beef procurement quantities to balance supply chain costs against fluctuating market prices. The decision variable is the weekly beef procurement quantity, planned for the upcoming 4 weeks. The objective is to maximize total expected profit while accounting for price volatility risks, where profit is calculated as total revenue minus total procurement cost and minus total inventory holding cost. Revenue is generated from sales at a constant price of $2600 per unit, with sales limited by market conditions and procurement decisions. Procurement cost equals the procurement quantity multiplied by the uncertain weekly beef price. The company must maintain adequate inventory levels to meet market requirements while avoiding excessive stockpiling. Any unsold inventory incurs a weekly holding cost of $150 per unit. The weekly procurement quantity must be non-negative and is limited by a weekly procurement capacity of 100 units. Additionally, total inventory cannot exceed 400 units due to storage limitations, and procurement decisions must consider budget constraints of $200,000 per week. The initial inventory is 50 units. The weekly average beef price is uncertain, derived from historical data. The goal is to determine an optimal procurement strategy that balances revenue generation against procurement costs and inventory expenses under price uncertainty.
